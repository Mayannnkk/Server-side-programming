Grand Central Terminal

Grand Central Terminal is a commuter rail terminal located at 42nd Street and Park Avenue in Midtown Manhattan. It is the largest train station in the world by number of platforms, with 44 platforms and 67 tracks. The terminal was built by and named for the New York Central Railroad and is now used by the Metro-North Railroad. The terminal is known for its beautiful architecture, including its main concourse, which features a large, ornate ceiling with a famous mural of the constellations.
