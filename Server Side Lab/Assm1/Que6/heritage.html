Heritage Sites

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. It was a gift from the people of France to the United States and is a symbol of freedom and democracy.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is a 102-story Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan. It was the world's tallest building for nearly four decades and is a popular tourist attraction.

Central Park

Central Park is an urban park in Manhattan, New York City. It is the most visited urban park in the United States and is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge is a hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge in New York City. It connects the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn and is a popular tourist attraction.

Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist destination, and entertainment center in the Midtown Manhattan section of New York City. It is known for its bright lights, billboards, and Broadway theaters.

Rockefeller Center

Rockefeller Center is a large complex of 19 commercial buildings covering 22 acres between 48th and 51st streets in Midtown Manhattan. It is a popular tourist destination and is home to the famous Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree.

Grand Central Terminal

Grand Central Terminal is a commuter rail terminal located at 42nd Street and Park Avenue in Midtown Manhattan. It is the largest train station in the world by number of platforms and is a popular tourist attraction.
